Lunch

Tacos

A popular and very good favorite of many people.

The first ingrediant you need is a tortilla. You need 1 cup of hamburger meat, 1/2 cup of shredded cheese, 1/2 cup of tomatoes and maybe some salsa if you are feeeling a little spicy. Put the tortilla on a plate then put all the ingediants on it.

Ricer Burrito

The ricer burrito is much like the taco. The only difference is after all the ingredients have been place on the tortilla, the burrito must be "slammed". Ie, make it as thin as possible. Take some nacho chesse and add racing stripe designs to the burrito and use toothpicks to make a spoiler.

Mexican Ricer Rice

This is a delicasy, much like a civic. Mess something up like exhaust, an you got rice. First, you need to cook up some rice. Then, add some stuff that makes it spicy. I would recommend crushed pepper.

Dinner

Chicken with Rice

The chicken with rice dinner is very simple to make. Perfect for a day when yo don't want to cook much.

Ingrediants: Chicken, rice, corn, onions, and tomatoes.

To make this dish, you need to cook up or roast some chicken to your liking. Cook the chicken and rice seperatly. When cooking the rice, add tomatoes, onions, and corn. Once both portions are done cooking, mix them together on a plate.

Mexican Chopped salad

Ingrediants: 12 ounces jicama, 12 ounces English cucumber, 1 pound cantaloupe, 1 pound watermelon, 1 pound honeydew, 4 ounces radishes, rinsed, 2 cups finely shredded red or green cabbage, 1 cup seedless red grapes, 1 firm-ripe avocado (8 to 10 oz.),2 tablespoons lemon or lime juice, 3/4 cup roasted salted peanuts

preparation: 1. Cut off and discard peel from jicama; rinse jicama. Rinse cucumber; trim and discard ends. Rinse cantaloupe, watermelon, and honeydew; cut off peels and scoop out seeds. Cut jicama, cucumber, and melons into 1/2-inch cubes. Trim and discard tops and roots from radishes; rinse radishes and thinly slice. Attractively arrange trimmed produce on a large platter along with cabbage and grapes. 2. Just before serving, pit and peel avocado. Cut into 1/2-inch cubes and coat with lemon juice; add to platter.
